Brute Force vs. Optimization

We can say that optimization is... read more

the process of making something as effective or functional as possible. In contrast, brute force methods involve exhaustive search strategies that try every possible option until a solution is found. While brute force can guarantee finding a solution, it often requires significant computational resources and time, making it impractical for large problem sizes. Optimization techniques, on the other hand, aim to find solutions more efficiently by using strategies that reduce the search space.
